Excluding flights, a three-day Rome trip runs roughly **€270–390 per person on a budget, €510–780 midrange, and €1,200 or more for comfort travel**. Accommodation and dining account for most of the spread; tickets and transport are comparatively small and predictable. ## How do you keep a Rome trip affordable?

Sleep just outside the historic core — one or two Metro stops out, or in Trastevere or Monti — where nightly rates drop while walking times barely change. Eat where the menu has no photos and no tout at the door, and stand at the bar for coffee, which is cheaper than table service. Buy a 48- or 72-hour transport pass only if you expect several rides a day; otherwise single tickets are cheaper. Book timed-entry tickets directly on the official sites to avoid resale mark-ups.
