Three days is enough to see the essential Rome — ancient Rome, the Vatican, and the historic centre — if you book the two big timed-entry sites ahead and keep each day in one area. This plan does exactly that, with verified prices and transport so you spend time walking, not queuing.

## How should you split three days in Rome?

Keep each day tight to one district so you walk between sights instead of crossing the city.

- **Day 1 — Ancient Rome.** Colosseum first thing on your timed slot, then the Roman Forum and Palatine Hill on the same combined ticket, finishing at Piazza Venezia.
- **Day 2 — Vatican.** Vatican Museums and the Sistine Chapel in the morning, then St Peter's Basilica. Book the museums early to avoid the longest lines.
- **Day 3 — Historic centre.** The Pantheon, Trevi Fountain, Piazza Navona and the Spanish Steps are all walkable in a loop, with a Trastevere dinner to end.

## How do you get from Fiumicino airport to the centre?

The **Leonardo Express** runs non-stop from Fiumicino to Roma Termini in about 32 minutes, at a fixed fare of roughly €14. The regional **FL1** line is cheaper but stops at Trastevere, Ostiense and Tiburtina rather than Termini, so it suits you only if your hotel is near one of those. A **fixed taxi fare** applies from the airport to any address inside the Aurelian Walls, which is worth it for late arrivals or heavy luggage.

## How do you skip the worst queues?

Timed, pre-booked entry is the single biggest time-saver in Rome. Arrive for the first slot of the day at the Colosseum and the Vatican Museums, when both are quietest. The Pantheon now charges a small entry fee and can be busy midday, so go early or near closing. Free sights like the Trevi Fountain and Spanish Steps are calmest before 9am.

## Getting around the city

Rome's centre is compact and best on foot, but the ATAC metro (lines A and B) and buses fill the gaps. A single BIT ticket costs €1.50 and is valid for 100 minutes including transfers; if you expect several rides a day, a 48- or 72-hour pass usually pays off. Validate every ticket on boarding.
